Back in DMC3, all weapons were unique with their design.
Not everyone had it’s own version of “High Time” launchers.

Such case was also in the SE for Vergil.
Vergil would get his own version of Rebellion, the “jack of all trades” weapon.

Was weaponing cycling good?

Back in DMC3 (with no mods), switching weapons was a toggle mechanic that allowed you to switch between 2 guns and two Devil Arms.
This meant that players could seamlessly switch between weapons without moving the thumbs from the face buttons.
This also saved the hassle from remebering what was your next weapon in the weapon cycle, because there was no cycle between 3 or more weapons.

Now, I am not against givin’ the player more than 3 weapons or more, I really do want devs to give players the option to access all weapons mid-game.
The problem that comes in games that heavily rely on a controller, is that you are very limited to the amount of buttons the players
can access and switch between with his thumbs.
I explained this thoroughly in the past that your thumbs press most of the buttons compared to your other fingers that press 1-2 buttons, i.e. the triggers and bumpers.

This is why, Doom Eternal on PC has no issue with weaopon swaping.
By a press of a single key once, I can switch to a weapon.
And, I can map that key to be closer or not to my WASD keys.

In DMC4, however, this ain’t possible with a controller and with keyboard sadly as well.
A player must cycle through Gilgamesh in order to equip Lucifer for the easy JC.
And giving the fact that in DMC4 has a delay between the time you finished pressing twice and the time it took the system to “load” Lucifer, it’s no wonder why it’s so hard for me and many other players to get used to weapon cycling in DMC4.

But just because DMC5 doesn’t have that problem, this doesn’t mean the idea of weapon cycling is good in the first place.
Let me re-state the 2 things I mentioned above

  • DMC3 allows you with a sinple press of a button to switch between weapons, i.e. “weapon toggle”
  • Doom Eternal gives you the option to assign one key to a single weapon

There’re no extra inputs to do before executing the actual input attack.
There’s no need to memorize which weapon will come next after X times cycling.

You just switch to the weapon, and input the attack.
Such case cannot exist in DMC4 and 5 because of this cycling.

And it doesn’t matter if you assign the cycling to the D-PAD or the trigger, the fact remains that you add an arbitrary challange to a simple thing as weapon switching mid combat.

That’s the reason why I hate this control design so much and why I prefer alternatives such as mapping weapons to the D-PAD like in GoW3.
For all the hate classic GoW games get from DMC fans, if there’s one thing that GoW3 does better than DMC4, is control design.

And just because you call it “steamlined” doesn’t mean it’s bad.
Ever since DmC Devil May Cry, fans of the series view “steamlined controls” as bad, me being one of them in the past.
But sometimes lagecy controls just can’t be carried over and tweaked.

DMC3 had the best controls for it’s game, and is still the best controls for Dante to this day.
But they do not work with more than 3 weapons or with D-PAD style-switching.
It’s simply isn’t the case.
